# Chapter 6: The External Fight

There is a version of this story in which I am the person who didn't try hard enough. Who gave up too soon. Who failed to find the right door or knock loudly enough or present the evidence in a way that moved someone to act.

I want to address that version directly, and then I want to put it to rest.

What follows is a list. Not a metaphor. Not a characterization. A documented record of every institution, every official, every congressional office, every attorney, and every newsroom I contacted between March 2023 and 2026 in an attempt to find someone — anyone — willing to look at what had been done to me and say: this matters, and we will do something about it.

I am going to name them. I am going to give you their titles and their dates. I am going to tell you what they said and what they didn't do.

Not out of bitterness — though I have earned the right to that. Out of precision. Because the pattern is the evidence. Because the accumulation of closed doors is itself a document. Because if you read this list and still believe I didn't try, I don't know what else I can show you.

## Part One — Federal Agencies and Officials

DHS Office of Inspector General
March 5, 2023
I faxed a confidential complaint to the DHS OIG two days after the coercive events of March 2nd. The transmission confirmation shows successful delivery at 9:57 AM. No substantive response was ever received. Two days later I was hospitalized.

The White House — Biden-Harris Administration
March and April 2023
I contacted the White House twice. Both times I received acknowledgments. The April response confirmed my correspondence had been forwarded to the "appropriate Federal agency for further action." No agency ever followed up with me directly.

Jodi Votel, EEO Counselor/Specialist — FAA Office of Civil Rights (ACT-9)
May 2023
I filed EEO complaint #2023-29988. Votel processed it and sent the Rights and Responsibilities documentation. On May 11th I withdrew the complaint. I wrote: "I am very fearful for my well-being if I move forward with it." Votel sent the withdrawal paperwork. I signed it. I later wrote to her: "I'd like to add that I signed that memorandum out of fear."

FAA Pre-Complaint — Timothy Wallace, Manager, AJR-22
June 22, 2023
I filed a second pre-complaint naming Timothy Wallace, Manager of Operational Security Initiatives, as the responsible management official. I alleged disability discrimination, harassment, and failure to provide reasonable accommodation. I elected ADR. No resolution was ever documented.

Sabina Frederick, Whistleblower Protection Coordinator — DOT OIG
January 11, 2024
Frederick contacted me by phone and then email asking me to clarify my allegations. I responded the same day with a detailed account of the March 2nd events. She responded hours later: "Based on a review of the information provided in your recent emails, we believe that DOT-OIG lacks oversight regarding your concerns. Additionally, we are not in a position to provide advice and anticipate no further action from our office concerning these matters."

Same day. Hours later. No further action.

FBI / Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
January 2024
I filed a complaint documenting the March 2nd events as coordinated internet crime — targeted ads on a federal device, alias account creation directives, financial coercion conducted through digital means. No response was ever documented.

Office of Special Counsel (OSC)
August 7, 2024
I filed four separate allegations: Abuse of Authority, Censorship Related to Scientific Research, Danger to Public Health, and Violation of Law, Rule or Regulation. The OSC website returned a notification indicating the FAA fell outside its jurisdiction. No active investigation resulted.

Michael Mills, AFDW/IG — Joint Base Andrews
July 2024
Mills contacted me after my SAF/IG complaint. He read the case. He asked careful questions and I gave careful answers. He said: "Right now, everything I'm seeing is either FAA or DoT… If we can't identify an Air Force issue, then we won't have a path forward." He could not identify an Air Force issue. He closed the case without referral.

Kimberly Mlinaz, Director of NDR — SAF/GCA
July 18, 2024
I contacted SAF's Negotiation and Dispute Resolution office separately. Mlinaz explained that NDR is an internal program — engagement requires a request from the IG. Mills would need to ask. Mills never asked. The ADR door I had been trying to open since March 2023 was closed procedurally by a person who never made a phone call.

Secretary of Transportation Pete Buttigieg
December 6, 2024
I sent a formal letter to Secretary Buttigieg at the Department of Transportation. I described the coercive directives, the alias account, the unauthorized medical access, the ongoing intimidation, and the failure of both the DHS and DOT OIGs to take action. I wrote: "The lack of response from your office has left me feeling unprotected and has seemingly emboldened those responsible."

No response was ever received.

I wrote: "I respectfully ask that you review the attached brief and consider referring this matter to the Task Force for further examination."

I contacted both sides of the aisle on the same day. The issue of a Pentagon official making a false statement to a United States Senator is not a partisan one. I wanted the record to show I understood that.

No response was documented from either office.

## Part Three — Legal Contacts

The attorneys were different from the institutions. The institutions had power and chose not to use it. The attorneys were being asked to take on a case that most civil litigation firms are not built to handle — a case involving national security, federal contractors, military officials, and a plaintiff whose psychiatric history had been deliberately weaponized against him.

I understand why most of them said no.

I am naming them anyway. Because the search itself was part of what was done to me. Every month spent finding another attorney, explaining everything again from the beginning, waiting for a response, and receiving a polite declination was another month the case aged and the statute of limitations shortened and the people responsible faced no consequence.

Bochetto & Lentz, P.C. — Philadelphia, PA
August 6, 2023
Contact form submitted. No representation offered.

Phillips & Cohen LLP — Washington, D.C.
March 7, 2024
Matthew Smith reviewed and declined. He wrote: "Our decision is not intended to suggest that a successful action cannot be maintained." He noted the urgency of statute of limitations.

Katz Banks Kumin — Washington, D.C.
July 12, 2024 — First Contact
Destiny Barber declined on behalf of the firm. Katz Banks Kumin is one of the most prominent whistleblower protection law firms in Washington, D.C. — a firm that specializes in exactly the kind of case I was bringing. They declined.

Eric Siegel Law, PLLC — Washington, D.C.
July 25, 2024
LeShante Henry contacted me via referral from the National Whistleblower Legal Defense and Education Fund. I provided a fact timeline and key documents. The firm reviewed and did not offer representation.

Skeen Law Offices — Charlottesville, VA
November 11–14, 2024
Michael Turiello reached out via Morgan and Morgan referral. We spoke by phone. I provided my case narrative and supporting documents. He wrote: "Despite my deepest sympathies for what you've experienced, we are unable to take your case."

Lundy Law — Philadelphia, PA
January 30, 2025
L. Leonard Lundy declined. The letter noted declining to represent "makes no representation as to the veracity or strength of this potential legal matter."

Government Accountability Project (GAP) — Washington, D.C.
January 29, 2025 — First Contact
Joanne Royce reviewed my Case Narrative and Legal Claims memo and declined representation. She wrote: "Our declining of services to you does not indicate that our staff thinks your claims to be unimportant or without merit."

Katz Banks Kumin — Washington, D.C.
July 3, 2025 — Second Contact
I contacted them again. Destiny Barber declined again. Same firm. Same result. One year later.

Relman Colfax PLLC — Washington, D.C.
July 9, 2025
Caroline Sullivan declined on behalf of the firm. She wrote: "We regret that we cannot represent all of the worthy and deserving individuals who come to us for help."

Government Accountability Project (GAP) — Washington, D.C.
April 15, 2026 — Second Contact
I applied again. They declined again. The intake team wrote: "our declining of services to you does not indicate that our staff thinks your concerns are unimportant or without merit."

Katz Banks Kumin — Washington, D.C.
April 1, 2026 — Third Contact
I contacted Lisa Banks, Partner, directly — subject line: "Potential Civil Claims — Federal Contractor Retaliation and Documented False Statement to U.S. Senator." Her out-of-office reply came back. No substantive response followed.

Three contacts. Three years. The same firm. The same result.

## Part Four — The Press

I also tried the press.

Not as a last resort. As a parallel channel. The same way I was filing complaints while reaching attorneys, I was reaching journalists. The formal record and the public record are different things. I wanted both.

Washington Post — Matea Gold
February 29, 2024
I emailed Matea Gold directly. I identified myself by name, explained that anonymity was impossible given the UAP connection, and listed the congressional offices I had already contacted. No response was documented.

6ABC Philadelphia
March 6, 2024
I contacted 6ABC by email and spoke with someone by phone. They gave me a contact number. Nothing came of it.

WJLA Washington
March 6 and March 23, 2024
I emailed the WJLA newsdesk twice. The first email bounced with a DNS error. I tried again on March 23rd. No substantive response was received.

CNN
June 6, 2024
I sent a tip via Proton Mail. I described the March 2023 coercive events, the alias account, the ESRI abstract, the retaliation. I wrote: "If there is somebody at CNN who does care, maybe forward this to that person." I noted that the Washington Post and the New York Times had not responded to prior outreach. No response was documented from CNN.

NBC News
July 2, 2025
I submitted a formal tip to NBC News investigative teams identifying myself as a former Leidos contractor whose UAP visualization work had been shared with ODNI. I described the coercive directives, the alias accounts, the psychiatric targeting, and the retaliation. NBC News Customer Care confirmed receipt and said they would forward it for review. No journalist followed up.

The Intercept, ProPublica, The Washington Post, The Guardian, CBC
July 2025
I BCC'd five investigative outlets on my urgent appeal to the UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights. The same story. The same documents. Five newsrooms. No response was documented from any of them.

The press, like the institutions, like the attorneys, received the information and returned the silence.

---

That is the list.

Federal agencies. Congressional offices. Attorneys. Newsrooms. Every door I knocked on between March 2023 and 2026. Every name, every title, every date, every polite declination and bureaucratic non-answer and carefully worded acknowledgment that amounted to nothing.

Not without merit. Worthy and deserving. Deepest sympathies. We will forward your message for review.

The words were always kind. The doors were always closed.

I kept knocking anyway. I am still knocking.

This book is a knock.
