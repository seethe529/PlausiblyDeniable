# Author's Note

This book is dedicated to the people who stayed.

To the friends who stayed — you know who you are. You were there during the worst of it and you made me feel heard when hearing me was not easy. That is not a small thing and I have not forgotten it.

To Kyle — you are a scientist and a rational one. You never reached for the easy explanation. You heard everything and you stayed. You made me feel heard without question, without hesitation. You were my anchor through all of it and you are the reason I came home.

---

To everyone who reads this — I want to say something before you turn the page.

There is a version of every story like this one that is easier to hold than the truth. It arrives quickly, it feels familiar, and it has the particular comfort of making the world make sense in a way that doesn't require you to change anything about how you understand it. It is the explanation that asks nothing of you.

I am asking you to resist it.

Not because the truth in these pages is comfortable. It isn't. Not because what happened to me is easy to accept. It isn't that either. I am asking you to resist the easy explanation because discomfort is not evidence of error. A story that makes you uneasy is not, by virtue of that unease, a story that isn't true. The people who designed what was done to me understood this. They built operations that depend on it. The architecture of plausibly deniable direction only works if the people around the target do exactly what they are designed to do, which is to look at each piece in isolation and conclude that nothing unusual has happened.

The documents in this book are real. The dates are real. The names are real. The contradiction at the center of this account — an eleven-message Air Force email chain on one side, a Pentagon official's denial to a United States Senator on the other — is not a matter of interpretation. It is a matter of record.

Read it carefully. Sit with what it adds up to. And when the easy explanation arrives, as it will, ask yourself who benefits from it being the one you choose.

*Ryan Charles Lingo, M.A.*
*April 2026*
